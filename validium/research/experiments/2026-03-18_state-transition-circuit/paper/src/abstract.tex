\begin{abstract}
Enterprise validium architectures require zero-knowledge circuits that prove
batches of state transitions over Sparse Merkle Trees (SMTs) while meeting
strict latency constraints. We present \textsc{ChainedBatchStateTransition},
a Circom circuit that proves sequential state updates over a Poseidon-based
SMT by chaining Merkle path verifications: each transaction's computed new
root becomes the next transaction's verified old root. We benchmark seven
configurations spanning tree depths 10, 20, and 32 with batch sizes 4, 8,
and 16 using Groth16 via SnarkJS~0.7.6 on commodity hardware. Our
measurements yield an exact per-transaction constraint formula of
$1{,}038 \cdot (d+1)$ R1CS constraints, where $d$ is the tree depth,
with zero residual error across all configurations. At depth~32 with
batch size~8, the circuit produces 274,027 constraints and generates a
Groth16 proof in 12.8~seconds. Extrapolation to the production target of
depth~32, batch~64 yields approximately 2.19~million constraints, requiring
an estimated 14--35~seconds with Rapidsnark but exceeding 120~seconds with
SnarkJS. Proof size remains constant at 803--807~bytes regardless of
circuit complexity. These results demonstrate that enterprise-grade
batch state transition proofs are feasible within sub-minute latency
budgets when paired with optimized native provers, and provide the
constraint scaling laws necessary for capacity planning in validium
deployments.
\end{abstract}
