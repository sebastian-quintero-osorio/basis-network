% abstract.tex -- Cross-Enterprise Verification Paper (RU-V7)

\begin{abstract}
Enterprise validium systems enable organizations to maintain private off-chain
state while submitting zero-knowledge proofs to a shared settlement layer, but
no existing protocol addresses the verification of \emph{cross-enterprise}
interactions---transactions recorded in two or more independent private state
trees---without breaking privacy boundaries. We present a cross-enterprise
verification protocol that uses a Groth16 cross-reference circuit (68,868
R1CS constraints) to prove the consistency of entries across two enterprise
sparse Merkle trees, bound by a Poseidon interaction commitment, with exactly
1 bit of information leakage per interaction (the existence of the
interaction---no keys, values, or transaction details are exposed). We
evaluate three verification approaches on an Avalanche L1 (Subnet-EVM):
Sequential verification achieves $1.41\times$ gas overhead over individual
enterprise verification, Batched Pairing verification achieves $0.64\times$
(below baseline through shared pairing computation), and Hub Aggregation
achieves $1.16\times$---all below the $2\times$ target. Cross-reference proof
generation takes an estimated 448~ms with rapidsnark (4,476~ms with snarkjs).
Scaling analysis across 2--50 enterprises confirms that batched pairing
maintains sub-$1\times$ overhead at all tested scales, while sequential
verification stays below $2\times$ for sparse interaction graphs but exceeds
the target in dense configurations (interactions $\gg$ enterprises). Compared
to Rayls (Parfin/Drex), which routes cross-enterprise interactions through a
metadata-visible hub chain, our protocol's hub coordinator sees only Groth16
proofs and Poseidon commitments. These results demonstrate that
privacy-preserving cross-enterprise verification is practical for enterprise
validium deployments with manageable gas overhead and sub-second proof
generation.
\end{abstract}
