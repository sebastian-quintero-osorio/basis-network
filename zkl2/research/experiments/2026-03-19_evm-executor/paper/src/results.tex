\subsection{Production zkEVM Architecture Comparison}

Table~\ref{tab:zkevm-comparison} summarizes the architectural choices of five
production systems.

\begin{table*}[htbp]
\caption{Production zkEVM and L2 Execution Engine Architectures}
\label{tab:zkevm-comparison}
\centering
\begin{tabular}{llllrr}
\toprule
\textbf{System} & \textbf{EVM Approach} & \textbf{Fork Base} & \textbf{Type} & \textbf{Proving Time/Batch} & \textbf{L2 TPS} \\
\midrule
Polygon zkEVM & Custom executor (C++) + zkASM & Not Geth (custom) & Type 3 & ${\sim}$190--200 s & ${\sim}$30--50 \\
Polygon CDK & Erigon fork (Go) & Erigon & Type 3 & ${\sim}$190--200 s & ${\sim}$30--50 \\
Scroll & Geth fork (scroll-geth) & go-ethereum & Type 2 & Target $<$30 s (2025) & Target 10K+ \\
zkSync Era & Custom VM (EraVM) & Not Geth & Type 4 & Variable & ${\sim}$100--300 \\
Optimism (op-geth) & Geth fork (minimal diff) & go-ethereum & Optimistic & N/A (no ZK) & ${\sim}$2{,}000 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table*}

A universal pattern emerges: no production zkEVM uses Geth's EVM directly for
proof generation. All systems separate the execution layer from the proving layer.
The execution engine (Geth, Erigon, or custom) processes transactions and generates
traces; a separate prover (C++ executor, zkASM interpreter, or custom circuit)
consumes these traces for proof generation.

\subsection{Geth Module Analysis}

The required modules (Table~\ref{tab:geth-modules}) total approximately
24{,}500~lines of code, representing ${\sim}$25\% of Geth's core Go code and
${\sim}$5\% of the total codebase (${\sim}$500{,}000+~LOC). The excluded modules
(Table~\ref{tab:geth-excluded}) account for ${\sim}$71{,}000~LOC of unnecessary
networking, consensus, mining, and CLI infrastructure.

The op-geth reference point is instructive: Optimism achieved a complete L2
execution layer with 16{,}881~lines added and 664~deleted, of which only
34~lines touch the EVM itself (precompile and caller overrides). The Basis
Network use case (execution + tracing, no L2 protocol features such as deposit
transactions or L1 cost computation) is expected to require a smaller
modification surface.
