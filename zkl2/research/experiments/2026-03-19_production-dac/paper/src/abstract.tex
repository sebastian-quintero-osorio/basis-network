\begin{abstract}
Enterprise zkEVM Layer~2 architectures require data availability guarantees
that simultaneously preserve commercial privacy and tolerate node failures.
We present a production Data Availability Committee (DAC) architecture that
combines AES-256-GCM symmetric encryption, Reed-Solomon $(5,7)$ erasure
coding, and Shamir $(5,7)$-secret sharing into a hybrid protocol achieving
computational data privacy with information-theoretic key secrecy. The
design extends our prior Validium DAC (RU-V6), which employed full Shamir
secret sharing over batch data, by restricting Shamir to the 32-byte AES
key and distributing erasure-coded ciphertext chunks to committee members.
Experimental evaluation on a Go implementation using the SIMD-optimized
\texttt{klauspost/reedsolomon} library demonstrates attestation latency of
4.5\,ms at 500\,KB and 8.9\,ms at 1\,MB---a 36$\times$ improvement over
the TypeScript-based RU-V6 baseline. Storage overhead converges to the
theoretical minimum of $1.40\times$ (versus $3.87\times$ for pure Shamir),
and data recovery completes in sub-millisecond time, representing a
$2{,}613\times$ speedup over Lagrange interpolation. Availability analysis
confirms that a $(5,7)$ committee with per-node reliability $p = 0.99$
achieves $99.997\%$ availability (4.5 nines). All hypothesis targets are
confirmed with margins between $19\times$ and $2{,}613\times$. We formalize
six invariants governing verifiable encoding, data recoverability, enterprise
privacy, attestation liveness, fallback safety, and availability guarantees.
\end{abstract}
